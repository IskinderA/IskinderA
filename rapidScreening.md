# Data-Driven Prediction of Molecular Transport in Polymer Systems
*A machine learning approach for predicting molecular diffusion and transport behavior across complex polymer–solute–solvent systems*

**Project duration:** September, 2025 – Present  

---

## Roles & Contributions

### My Contributions

- Developed data-driven models to predict **solute diffusivity** in polymer systems using existing curated experimental datasets  

- Designed and implemented **machine learning frameworks** (quantile random forests and neural networks) to estimate transport behavior across polymer–solute–solvent systems  

- Engineered physically informed input features based on **material properties, swelling behavior, and molecular descriptors**  

- Performed model validation using cross-validation strategies and statistical analysis to assess predictive performance and uncertainty  

- Integrated mechanistic understanding of transport phenomena with data-driven modeling to improve interpretability and applicability  

### Collaborative Contributions  

- Addtional contributions anticipated to dataset curation/augmentation/improvement and harmonization across multiple experimental sources  

- Collaborations anticipated with interdisciplinary teams to further align data-driven prediction outputs with **empiricial observations and application-relevant scenarios**  

---

## Key Findings (Tentative)

- Demonstrated that machine learning models can predict **order-of-magnitude diffusivity** across diverse polymer systems without system-specific calibration  

- Established that combining **physically informed descriptors** with data-driven methods improves model robustness and interpretability  

- Quantified uncertainty in transport predictions using **quantile-based modeling approaches**, enabling more reliable evaluation of model outputs  

- Developed a framework that complements traditional physics-based models, enabling **rapid screening of transport behavior** across large parameter spaces
