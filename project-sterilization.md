# Transport and Reaction Modeling in Polymeric Systems for Sterilization and Material Performance
* A physics-based framework linking material properties, geometry, and process conditions to molecular transport, exposure, and system-level performance *


**Project duration:** November 2022 – Present  

---

## Roles & Contributions

### My Contributions

- Developed semi-analytic **reaction–diffusion models** to describe sterilant transport across a range of material geometries, with verification using in-house numerical implementations  

- Quantified **chemical migration** in single- and multi-layer polymer systems, including **semi-crystalline** (e.g., polyetherurethane) and **amorphous** (e.g., silicone) materials relevant to medical device sterilization  

- Contributed to microbial inactivation modeling and analysis to evaluate **sterilant efficacy** at doses predicted by transport models  

- Interpreted and post-processed in-house experimental datasets to extract **transport parameters** and validate model predictions  

### Collaborative Contributions  

- Supported experimental efforts for diffusion **measurement and validation** across multiple polymer systems  

- Collaborated with microbiologists and industry partners to evaluate **sterilization cycle parameters and performance**  

- Integrated modeling and experimental findings to inform discussions around **safer and more effective sterilization practices**  

---

## Key Findings

- Developed a mechanistic framework linking **time-dependent concentration (∂C/∂t)** to **diffusion (∂²C/∂x²)** and **reaction kinetics (R)** in polymer systems with realistic boundary conditions  

- Demonstrated that the **finite-volume reaction–diffusion model** can be semi-analytically verified and used to predict sterilant penetration depth, exposure time, and delivered dose  

- Established how material properties, geometry, and process conditions jointly govern **sterilant transport and microbial inactivation outcomes**  

- Provided a modeling framework that can guide **design of sterilization cycles** and support broader efforts to reduce reliance on environmentally hazardous sterilants
